📊 How to Read These Heatmaps

🔢 What the Numbers Mean

  • Mean Difference (±0.XXX): How much the emotion probability changed between personality A and personality B. Positive = increased, negative = decreased.
  • Asterisks (***): Indicates statistical significance (p < 0.05 with FDR correction). More asterisks = stronger evidence.
  • Cell Color: Warmer colors (red) = stronger positive effect; cooler colors (blue) = stronger negative effect.

⬜ Why Some Cells Are Blank

  • Missing Data: Not all model-personality-context combinations were tested in the dataset.
  • NaN Values: These cells contain no observations, so no statistical test was possible.
  • What It Means: A blank cell is different from a cell showing a small effect—blank means "untested," not "no effect."

💡 Interpretation Example

A cell showing "+0.045 ***" means: When models received personality instruction A vs B, the emotion probability increased by 4.5 percentage points, and this difference is statistically significant. A blank cell in the same row/column means that particular model-context combination wasn't in our dataset.
